# Gharib Analytics Discovery & Planning Document
**Version:** v0.1  
**Date:** 29 June 2026  
**Purpose:** Client-facing website, portfolio projects, niche selection, and client acquisition strategy
---
## 1. Executive Summary
- The website should not present Gharib Analytics as only a dashboard-building service. It should position the business as a trusted analytics partner that helps small organisations understand what is working, what is leaking money, and what to improve next.
- The first website version should focus on credibility, practical business outcomes, and a low-risk call-to-action: “Book a free data/reporting audit.”
- The strongest initial niches are healthcare-adjacent small businesses and education/learning businesses. Gyms and fitness studios are useful secondary targets. Restaurants and hospitality should be approached later or only with a very specific profitability/operations angle.
- The two initial portfolio projects should be an Education Centre Growth Dashboard and a Clinic Performance Dashboard, both using synthetic or anonymised data and presented as before-and-after transformation stories.

## 2. Core Positioning
### Simple positioning statement
Gharib Analytics helps healthcare, education and local service businesses turn messy business data into clear dashboards, automated reports and practical decisions.

### Stronger client-facing promise
I help small businesses turn scattered data into clear dashboards, better decisions, and measurable actions — without needing to hire a full-time analyst.

### What to avoid
Avoid leading with technical wording such as “I build Power BI dashboards.” That sounds like a technical deliverable rather than a business outcome.

### What to lead with instead
Lead with business problems: missed opportunities, unclear performance, manual reporting, disconnected systems, no-shows, poor visibility, weak conversion tracking, and decisions made from gut feeling rather than evidence.

## 3. Website Strategy

| Client Type | Examples | Priority | Reasoning |

| Healthcare-adjacent businesses | Dental clinics, physios, aesthetics clinics, opticians, private therapy clinics | High | Strong fit with healthcare/NHS/private healthcare credibility. Clear value around bookings, no-shows, service revenue, utilisation and patient/customer source analysis. |

## 5. Portfolio Projects
### Project 1: Education Centre Growth Dashboard
- Purpose: show education providers that Gharib Analytics understands enquiries, enrolments, attendance, student progress, tutor utilisation and course revenue.
- Recommended pages: Executive Summary; Enquiries & Conversion; Student Attendance & Retention; Tutor/Class Utilisation; Revenue by Course/Centre; Student Progress Snapshot; Action List.
- Best story angle: from disconnected spreadsheets and manual tracking to a weekly owner dashboard with clear growth and retention actions.

### Project 2: Clinic Performance Dashboard
- Purpose: show healthcare-adjacent businesses that Gharib Analytics can translate operations into commercial and service decisions.
- Recommended pages: Executive Summary; Bookings, Attendance & No-Shows; Service Revenue & Profitability; Staff/Room Utilisation; Patient or Customer Source Analysis; Demand Forecast; Action List.
- Best story angle: from scattered booking/service data to a clear view of demand, missed revenue, no-shows, utilisation and next actions.

**Data safety rule:** Use synthetic or anonymised data only. Make this clear on the website to demonstrate professionalism, especially for healthcare and education examples.

## 7. Client Acquisition Approach
**Outreach principle:** Do not ask “Do you need a dashboard?” Instead, speak to business pain: unclear bookings, no-shows, lost revenue, manual reporting, scattered data, poor visibility and lack of weekly decision-making clarity.

1. Build a prospect list from Google Maps and LinkedIn: 30 clinics, 30 education centres, and 20 gyms/fitness studios.
2. Send personalised outreach that offers a free 20-minute reporting/data audit, not a generic sales call.
3. Use short Loom/video audits for selected prospects when there is a visible opportunity.
4. Partner with accountants, bookkeepers, web designers and marketing agencies who already serve small businesses.
5. Use the website CTA to route people into a free audit rather than an open-ended contact form.

## 8. Discovery Questions for Next Session
1. Should Gharib Analytics feel like a solo senior consultant, boutique analytics studio, future agency, or productised service business?
2. What client size is most attractive: 1–10 staff, 10–50 staff, or 50–250 staff?
3. Which first niche feels most exciting: clinics, education, gyms, restaurants, local services, or a mix?
4. How many hours per week can realistically be invested over the next 8–12 weeks?
5. What is the first income milestone: £300–£500 project, £1,000 project, £2,000+ project, first retainer, or website live first?
6. Which services are most attractive to sell: dashboards, audits, automation, KPI consulting, retainers, website analytics, CRM/customer analysis, or marketing performance analysis?
7. Should the public tool positioning lead with Power BI, Looker Studio, Tableau, or tool-agnostic delivery?
8. Should portfolio projects be dashboards only, dashboards plus case studies, fake business pages, or full before-and-after transformation stories?
9. Should prices be shown as “starting from” or kept enquiry-based for now?
10. Should the site strongly feature Khalid Gharib personally, or make Gharib Analytics feel bigger than one person?

## 9. Current Working Decisions
- Brand direction: professional, practical, trustworthy, and clear rather than overly futuristic or agency-like.
- Initial CTA: Book a free data/reporting audit.
- Initial portfolio projects: Education Centre Growth Dashboard and Clinic Performance Dashboard.
- Initial niche focus: healthcare-adjacent and education/learning businesses.
- Secondary niche: gyms, wellness and appointment-based businesses.
- Lower initial priority: restaurants and hospitality unless the offer is very practical and ROI-specific.
- Pricing direction: enquiry-based at first, supported by named packages.
- Best portfolio format: dashboard plus written case study plus before-and-after story.
