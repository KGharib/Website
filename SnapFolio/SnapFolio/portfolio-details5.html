  • Project date: 2020
  • Project URL: NBA Hypothesis Testing

Project Overview

investigated an NBA statistics dataset sourced from Kaggle, applying statistical analysis to explore three key research questions. Using established data cleaning and exploratory data analysis (EDA) techniques, we prepared the dataset, identified patterns and trends, and formulated data-driven hypotheses based on our findings.

A few of these Hypotheses were:

  • Is There a significant difference in player performance when playing Home vs Away
  • Do teams in the Western Conference face a higher statistical barrier to playoff qualification than teams in the Eastern Conference over the last 16 seasons?
  • To what extent does the presence of one or more All-Star–level players (measured by a player score greater then 40) influence team win outcomes?

    • We found that:
  • No significant differences observed in key performance metrics such as points scored, assists, and rebounds. when playing Home VS Away
  • Teams in the Western Conference generally face a higher statistical barrier to playoff qualification compared to teams in the Eastern Conference, as the teams playing in the west are generally higher performing and more.
  • The presence of All-Star–level players has a positive influence on team win outcomes, with teams featuring such players exhibiting higher win rates compared to those without.

    • Key Features

    End-to-End Statistical Analysis & Hypothesis Testing

    Designed and executed multiple hypothesis tests across 18 NBA seasons, translating vague basketball questions into well-defined null and alternative hypotheses

    Advanced Data Cleaning & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

    Applied robust data cleaning, feature selection, and EDA techniques to prepare a large, multi-season sports dataset. Identified trends, distributions, and anomalies to validate assumptions before testing, ensuring statistical conclusions were methodologically sound.

    Data-Driven Evaluation of Competitive Dynamics

    Quantified abstract concepts such as “playoff difficulty” and “player impact” using measurable metrics (e.g. win thresholds, DF_SCORE). Demonstrated the ability to convert real-world business or domain questions into testable, quantitative frameworks.

    Insightful Interpretation & Communication of Results

    Went beyond test outputs to provide clear, contextual interpretations of results—explaining why differences exist (e.g. conference competitiveness, superstar influence) and what they imply. This highlights strong analytical storytelling and stakeholder-ready communication skills.
